🔋 **New Project: Driving the Future — EV Adoption Analysis in India**

I built an end-to-end data analytics project analyzing India's electric vehicle adoption landscape (2018–2025) and forecasting growth through 2030 — from raw data cleaning all the way to an executive-ready Power BI dashboard.

**What's inside:**
🧹 A full data cleaning pipeline (Python + SQL) — deduplication, state-name standardization, missing-value imputation, IQR-based outlier treatment
🗃️ Advanced SQL — CTEs, window functions, YoY growth, ranking, market share analysis
🐍 Python forecasting — an ensemble of log-linear regression and a custom damped Holt's-trend model, projecting EV registrations 5 years out with confidence intervals
📊 A 6-page Power BI dashboard (dark theme) covering Executive Overview, State-wise Analysis, Vehicle Category breakdown, Charging Infrastructure gaps, Time Series trends, and a Forecast page — with drill-through, bookmarks, and custom tooltips

**A few insights that stood out:**
→ Two-wheelers still dominate volume (~63% share), but buses and commercial EVs are growing the fastest — fleet electrification is the next frontier.
→ The states leading in raw EV volume (UP, Maharashtra, West Bengal) are also the states with the widest charging-infrastructure gap — demand is outpacing supply exactly where it matters most.
→ The model projects national EV registrations growing roughly 4-5x by 2030, with growth *rate* gradually normalizing as the market matures — a classic S-curve adoption pattern.

This was a great exercise in building a project the way I'd approach it for a real stakeholder — clean data foundations, defensible analysis, and a dashboard designed to answer "so what do we do about it," not just "what happened."
